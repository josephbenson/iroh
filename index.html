The Gap

The Problem

Talent

  • Nigerian engineers are high-agency, world-class, and invisible to the capital that could fund them
  • No single company here has the hardware, software, and AI depth to staff niche R&D problems
  • The programs that do exist get bypassed — talent gets poached, and the platform that built them captures nothing

Data

  • Nobody has reliable ground-truth data on the Nigerian market — not input costs, not agent density, not real infrastructure
  • Real resources — government labs, mini-grids, long-term mortgages — sit unmapped and invisible
  • Without that data, diaspora capital and foreign investors are deciding blind

The Mechanism

How It Works

  1. Layer 1

    Data Collection

    Youth groups already on the ground — NYSC cohorts, university clubs, JCI chapters — collect narrow, specific datasets. They're paid in access to the diaspora network, not cash.

  2. Layer 2

    Skills Pipeline

    Data collectors move into the fellowship. Engineers qualify through one free, curated learning path — we're not building a curriculum, just a filter for who's ready.

  3. Layer 3

    R&D Marketplace

    Companies post real problems with real budgets attached. Engineers deliver working outputs. Universities supervise for quality, so nothing ships unverified.
